Antonio Meucci

The man who is the true inventor of telephone


Antonio Meucci's Life

Inventor and Italian scientist born in Florence in 1808 and died in New York in 1889. He was the inventor of the telephone, even though historically the "fatherhood" of the device was attributed to the American of Scottish-born Alexander Graham Bell. Meucci had studied mechanical engineering in his native Florence and in the Decade of the 1930s emigrated to Cuba in search of fortune. He had no luck and after working for some years as a stagehand and healer in Havana returned to journey, this time bound for New York. In Cuba, while working with rheumatic patients, that small electric shocks applied to alleviate the pain, he discovered that the transformation of sound vibrations into electrical impulses allowed to transmit voice to distance, through a cable.

Meucci's First Telephone

That same year, the demand for patent of the device requested in New York and baptized with the name of teletrofono. To renew the demand document you had to pay ten dollars each year. The Italian scientist fulfilled this obligation the first two, but in 1874 he could not get the money and lost their legal rights to the invention. Meucci then sought financial support and presented his teletrofono to Western Union, but the famous Telegraph Company rejected the offer. In 1876 and for surprise and unfortunately for Meucci, the physicist of Scottish-born Graham Bell patented an apparatus of voice transmission which he called phone. The Italian scientist claimed their rights in the courts but died without anyone to recognize his contribution to one of the major inventions of the 20th century. For more than one century remained the error attribute the paternity of the phone to Graham Bell until finally, on 11 June 2002, the Congress of the United States officially recognized Meucci as its true inventor.

Antonio Meucci's Chronology

  • 1808 - Born in Florence, Italy
  • 1837 - Married Esterre Mochi, who was a dress designer
  • 1834 - He designed an apparatus which would help people to communicate in the theatre
  • 1835 - He travelled to Cuba along with his wife
  • 1850 - He and his wife left Cuba and moved to the United States
  • 1870 - He invented the first telephone(teletrofono), even though historically the "fatherhood" of the device was attributed to Alexander Graham Bell.
  • 1871 - He submitted a patent caveat for his telephonic device to the U.S. Patent Office
  • 1874 - He could not get the money and lost their legal rights to the invention. Meucci did not have the money to file a patent.
  • 1876 - Graham Bell created a patent of Meucci’s telephonic invention and Bell had already got a patent.
  • 1877 - -Graham Bell established Bell Telephone (American Telephone & Telegraph Company (AT&T)), the first telephone company in the USA
  • 1877-1886 - He claimed his rights in the courts and won the case but anyone to recognize his contribution to one of the major inventions of the 20th century.
  • 1889 - Died at Age: 81 in the USA- New York
  • 2002 - The U.S. House of Representatives also honoured Meucci in a resolution in 2002
  • 2008 - Italian Ministry of Cultural Heritage and Activities supported celebrations of Meucci's 200th birthday in 2008 using the title "Inventore del telefono" (Inventor of the telephone).
